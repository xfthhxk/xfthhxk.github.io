Why?

Enterprise Software

Software in an organization generally consists of user centric applications and unsupervised processes. As the organization grows and more automation is required, unsupervised processes will often start to out number user centric applications.

Examples of automation may include:

  • Every Monday charge customers for services used
  • Download customer reviews from the Internet

Cron: The Goto Solution

On Linux systems, cron is often the first step in automating processes based on time. cron is ubiquitous and relatively simple to use. However, cron does have drawbacks.
  • No centralized management of distributed processes.
  • No dependency management among jobs.
  • No visibility and tracking of failures.
  • No history of executions.
